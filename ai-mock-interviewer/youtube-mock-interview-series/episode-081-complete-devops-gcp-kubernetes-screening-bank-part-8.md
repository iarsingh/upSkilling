## Question 9

Interviewer:
How do you prioritize incidents?

Pause the video and answer this question aloud.

Senior Associate answer:
A strong answer should use a clear STAR structure: situation, responsibility, action, and measurable result. Cover severity, impact, triage, ownership, communication cadence, mitigation, validation, postmortem actions, and follow-up automation.

Detailed interview preparation:
Use the STAR method: Situation, Task, Action, Result. Start with the business or user impact, explain your responsibility, describe the technical steps and communication steps you took, and end with measurable results.

What to include:
- Context: system size, severity, users affected, environment, and why it mattered.
- Investigation: metrics, logs, traces, alerts, recent changes, dependencies, and hypotheses checked.
- Action: mitigation first, then root-cause analysis, long-term fix, validation, and rollback/failover if needed.
- Communication: incident updates, stakeholder alignment, escalation, timeline notes, and handoff.
- Result: reduced MTTR, improved SLO, fewer alerts, lower cost, better reliability, or stronger security.

Key focus for this question:
Emphasize impact assessment, incident command, mitigation, stakeholder communication, RCA, and prevention. Avoid a vague answer like "I checked logs and fixed it." Interviewers expect a structured story with evidence, tradeoffs, prioritization, and follow-up prevention.

Sample answer structure:
"In one production situation, we noticed [impact]. I was responsible for [role]. I first [mitigation], then investigated using [signals/tools]. The root cause was [cause]. We fixed it by [technical fix], validated with [metrics/tests], and prevented recurrence by [automation/runbook/alert/policy]. The result was [measurable improvement]."

Senior answer structure:
Use this structure: direct answer -> real project example -> risk/tradeoff -> validation -> senior ownership point.
