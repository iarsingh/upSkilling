## Opening

Hi everyone, welcome back to the Data Science Mock Interview Practice series.

In today's episode, we are practicing Product Metrics and Business Case Study. Questions increase from foundation level to principal-level judgment.

Pause after each question and answer aloud. Clarify definitions and assumptions, show your method, discuss risks and limitations, and finish with how the result would support a decision.

---

## Question 1

Experience level: 0-1 year | Foundation

Interviewer:
What makes a useful product metric, and how do vanity metrics differ from actionable metrics?

Pause the video and answer this question aloud.

Senior Associate answer guide:
Clarify the product and decision, define a metric tree, segment the data, distinguish correlation from causation, quantify impact and uncertainty, and recommend an executable next step. For this 0-1 year | Foundation question, the answer should demonstrate fundamental correctness and clear reasoning. Include a concrete example, the most important assumption or failure risk, and the evidence you would use to validate the conclusion.

Senior answer structure:
Use this structure: definition -> simple example -> basic validation.

Scenario-based practice:
Apply the question to a realistic product or business situation. Explain the context, data, method, tradeoff, validation, recommendation, and ownership for: What makes a useful product metric, and how do vanity metrics differ from actionable metrics?

What interviewer checks:
They are checking fundamental correctness and clear reasoning, plus whether you connect technical analysis to a defensible business decision.
